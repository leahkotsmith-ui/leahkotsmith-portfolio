My Hobbies & Interests

Reading

Reading is one of my favorite pastimes because it allows me to explore new worlds and ideas. I enjoy a mix of fiction and nonfiction, and I often find myself drawn to stories that make me think in new ways. Reading has helped me develop patience, focus, and creativity. One of my favorite experiences was reading a book series with my roommate and getting to experience all the twists and turns of the plot and characters with her.

Baking

Baking has always been a creative outlet for me. I love experimenting with recipes and adding my own twist to classic desserts. One of my favorite memories is baking cookies with my family during the holidays. We would always make gingerbread cookies and have competitions of whose were the best. Baking is important to me because it combines precision with creativity, and I enjoy seeing the happiness my baked goods bring to others.

Traveling

Exploring and traveling to new places is exciting because it challenges me to step outside of my comfort zone, try new things, and meet people from different backgrounds. Traveling is important to me because it broadens my perspective on the world. This past summer, I had the chance to study abroad in Scandinavia. Traveling through cities like Copenhagen and Stockholm gave me a new appreciation for sustainability, culture, and design.

Drawing & Crafting

I also enjoy drawing and crafting in my free time. Whether it's sketching in a notebook or making wooden crafts, this hobby lets me express myself visually. A small but memorable project was creating a personalized chalk drawing for my grandma; it reminded me how powerful creativity can be in showing care and thoughtfulness.

Playing Games

I love playing games, from card games to board games. Games are a ton of fun, but they also sharpen problem-solving and teamwork skills. Games are significant to me because they helped me learn how to communicate and strategize and brought me closer to my friends and family. Some of my favorite memories involve playing a variety of games with people I am close to on games nights, where competition and laughter always go hand in hand.

Music

Music has been a big part of my life for many years. I played clarinet for eight years, which taught me discipline, teamwork, and the joy of performing as part of a group. Beyond playing, I also love listening to music and going to concerts. There's something special about the energy of live performances. For me, the connection between the musicians and the audience is unforgettable. Music is important to me because it can instantly change my mood, bring back memories, and create a sense of community.
